About This Project

📌 Project Details
Course Natural Language Processing (B.Tech)
Project Title Semantic Drift Analysis Over Time
Dataset Stanford / Google Ngram SGNS Embeddings
Source snap.stanford.edu/historical_embeddings
Time Span 1800s – 2000s (decade granularity)
Vocabulary ~65,000 words per decade
Vector Dim 300-dimensional SGNS
⚙ Methodology
  1. Data Loading — Pre-trained SGNS word vectors from each decade loaded via NumPy
  2. Cosine Distance — Drift score = 1 − cosine_similarity(v_t₁, v_t₂)
  3. Neighbour Shift — Jaccard overlap of top-K nearest neighbours between eras
  4. Procrustes Alignment — SVD-based orthogonal rotation to align embedding spaces
  5. t-SNE Projection — 2-D visualisation of neighbourhood structure
  6. Global Ranking — All shared high-frequency words ranked by drift score
  7. Concept Tracking — Pairwise similarity between concepts across decades
🔬 Types of Semantic Change
Broadening

Word gains new, wider meaning (broadcast: seeds → media)

Narrowing

Word restricts to a subset of its old meaning

Amelioration

Word gains positive connotation over time (nice)

Pejoration

Word gains negative connotation over time (awful)

Metaphorical Extension

Concrete meaning → abstract/figurative (virus: biology → computing)

Stable

Word has consistent semantic neighbourhood across time
