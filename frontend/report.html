Semantic Drift Report Generator

Discovers which words drive a sentence's meaning change across historical decades — extending the Top-K Discovery Paradigm from single words to full sentences using counterfactual contribution scoring and domain transition analysis.

📝 Input — Sentence, Idiom, or Phrase
Sample phrases:
1 · Executive Summary
2 · Quantitative Overview
3 · Top-K Discovery Ranking words ranked by counterfactual contribution to sentence drift
Contribution Score = how much sentence drift drops if this word's embedding had stayed frozen (counterfactual). A high contribution means this word is the primary driver of the sentence's meaning shift.
4 · Per-Word Reasoning discovery annotation for top-K drivers
5 · Stable Anchor Words words that held the sentence's meaning together

Anchor words are content words whose individual drift AND counterfactual contribution are both low — they serve as semantic constants that gave the sentence continuity across the eras studied.

6 · Domain Transition Map

Summary of which semantic domains the sentence's vocabulary migrated between. Based on nearest-neighbor lexical overlap with 10 curated domain seed sets.

Semantic Drift Analysis · B.Tech NLP Major Project
Sentence-level extension of Lee et al., "Evaluating Semantic-Change Discovery: A Top-K Paradigm for Contextualized Representations", EMNLP 2025
Dataset: Stanford/Google Ngram SGNS Embeddings (1800–1990)
